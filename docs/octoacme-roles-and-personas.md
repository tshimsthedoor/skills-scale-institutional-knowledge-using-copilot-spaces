# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Scrum Master

### Role Summary
Scrum Masters facilitate Agile processes and ceremonies, coaching the team on Agile practices. They ensure that blockers are identified and removed, enabling the team to deliver value efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate sprint planning, daily standups, and retrospectives
- Coach the team on Agile and Scrum best practices
- Work closely with PM and PdM to ensure alignment and remove blockers
- Shield the team from distractions and interruptions
- Promote continuous improvement and team health

### Goals
- Maximize team velocity and predictability
- Foster a culture of collaboration and transparency
- Ensure impediments are resolved quickly
- Improve sprint-over-sprint performance

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups to track progress and identify blockers
- Sprint planning and retrospective facilitation
- One-on-ones with team members to address concerns
- Coordination with PM and stakeholders on dependencies

---

## Release Manager

### Role Summary
Release Managers coordinate deployments and release schedules, ensuring that releases are executed smoothly and according to plan. They manage the release calendar and communicate deployment windows to all stakeholders.

### Responsibilities
- Coordinate deployments and release schedules
- Ensure release notes and QA steps are followed
- Collaborate with PM, Developers, QA, and Support on release readiness
- Manage the release calendar and communicate deployment windows
- Track release metrics and post-deployment issues

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, on-time releases
- Minimize deployment risks and rollback incidents
- Ensure clear communication of release status to stakeholders
- Continuously improve release processes

### Typical Communication
- Weekly release planning meetings
- Deployment notifications and status updates
- Release notes and changelogs
- Post-release retrospectives and incident reviews

---

## Technical Writer

### Role Summary
Technical Writers document processes, features, and user guides, ensuring that knowledge is accessible and up-to-date. They support onboarding and ongoing knowledge sharing across the organization.

### Responsibilities
- Document processes, features, and user guides
- Interact with PM, PdM, Developers, and QA to ensure accuracy
- Maintain documentation quality, consistency, and accessibility
- Support onboarding materials and training resources
- Review and update documentation regularly

### Goals
- Provide clear, accurate, and accessible documentation
- Reduce time to onboard new team members
- Enable self-service for users and support teams
- Maintain documentation that evolves with the product

### Typical Communication
- Feature review meetings with PdM and Developers
- Documentation reviews and feedback sessions
- Collaboration on release notes and user guides
- Regular audits and updates of existing documentation

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers define user interfaces and experience flows, ensuring that products are intuitive and meet user needs. They conduct research and usability testing to validate design decisions.

### Responsibilities
- Define user interfaces and experience flows
- Work with PdM, Developers, and Stakeholders to validate designs
- Conduct user research and usability testing
- Create wireframes, prototypes, and design specifications
- Ensure design consistency and accessibility standards

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, user-centered designs
- Validate designs through research and testing
- Reduce friction and improve user satisfaction
- Maintain design system consistency across products

### Typical Communication
- Design review sessions with PdM and Developers
- User research findings and usability test reports
- Wireframes, prototypes, and design specifications
- Collaboration on feature requirements and acceptance criteria

---

## Support Lead

### Role Summary
Support Leads manage incoming requests, bug triage, and communication with stakeholders. They ensure that critical issues are escalated quickly and provide a vital feedback loop from customers to the product team.

### Responsibilities
- Manage incoming support requests and bug triage
- Ensure critical issues are escalated quickly to the right teams
- Coordinate with Release Manager and PM on post-release issues
- Provide feedback loop from customers to product team
- Track support metrics and identify trends

### Goals
- Minimize customer downtime and resolution time
- Ensure high-quality customer support experience
- Provide actionable insights to improve product quality
- Build strong relationships with customers and internal teams

### Typical Communication
- Daily triage meetings with engineering and QA
- Escalation notifications for critical issues
- Weekly support metrics and trend reports
- Customer feedback summaries and feature requests

---

## Automation Engineer

### Role Summary
Automation Engineers create and maintain CI/CD pipelines and automated testing frameworks. They work to improve deployment automation and ensure reliable, repeatable releases through infrastructure as code.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain CI/CD pipelines and automated testing frameworks
- Collaborate with Developers for optimal testing coverage
- Work with Release Manager and QA for reliable releases
- Improve deployment automation and infrastructure as code
- Monitor and optimize build and deployment performance

### Goals
- Achieve fast, reliable automated deployments
- Maximize test automation coverage and efficiency
- Reduce manual toil and deployment errors
- Enable developers to self-service deploy with confidence

### Typical Communication
- Sprint planning for automation improvements
- Pipeline failure notifications and incident response
- Collaboration with Developers on testing strategies
- Infrastructure and deployment status updates
